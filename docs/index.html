PROJECTS

NASA & CLAWS-UM

Developing AR interfaces for NASA astronauts

I was the president of the Collaborative Lab for Advancing Work in Space, a project team at the University of Michigan.

We were selected by NASA to design and develop augmented reality interfaces to assist astronauts in critical mission tasks such as
  • navigation
  • search and rescue
  • task management
  • geological sampling
  • tracking vitals
  • messaging and communication

Within the team, I worked as both the AR development lead and the project manager. My roles constantly shifted between focusing on AR development - to software integration - to UX design - and to whole team management.

Bosch (Autonomous Parking Team)

Streamlining in-vehicle testing with automation

During my 2022 summer internship at Bosch, I developed a Python GUI to automate the process to flash software to microcontrollers (ECUs).

To complete the task, I
  1. gained experience with hardware-in-the-loop testing
  2. learned how to use embedded software development tools
  3. led meetings to discuss obstacles in the flashing process
  4. investigated the root cause for a common flashing error
  5. uncovered cybersecurity incompatibilities as the root cause issue
  6. developed a workaround to the cybersecurity issue
  7. created a GUI to automate the flashing process and the workaround
